    Alice Lynne "Lindy" Chamberlain-Creighton is a New Zealand-born woman who was falsely convicted in one of Australia's most senior murder crimes trials. Accused of killing her 9-week-old daughter, Azaria, while camping in Uluru in 1980, she claimed to have seen a dingo leave the tent where Azaria slept. The prosecution is factorial and depends on forensic evidence. Chamberlain was convicted on October 29, 1982 and her appeals to the Australian Federal Court and the Supreme Court of Australia were dismissed. On February 7, 1986, after discovering new evidence, Chamberlain was released from prison after being granted parole. She and her husband Michael Chamberlain, co-offenders, were formally pardoned in 1987 and their sentences were overturned by the Northern Territory Supreme Court in 1988. In 1992, the Australian government received compensation of $ 1.3 million to Chamberlain.

    Career

    High School Career

    Odell played wide receiver, quarterback, running back, and cornerback for the Greenies football team.

    During his junior year, he caught 45 passes for 743 yards and ten touchdowns and added three more scores on the ground.

    When he was a senior, he recorded 50 catches for 1,010 yards and 19 touchdowns and became one of the two players in the school history to record 1,000 receiving yards in a season.

    During high school, he was an all-district selection in his junior and senior years in basketball and also played in the 2011 US Army All-American Bowl.

    College Career

    Odell debuted against the Oregon Ducks on September 3, 2011, and started nine of 14 games in his first year at LSU.

    He scored his first college touchdown against West Virginia Mountaineers in a 52-yard reception from Jarrett Lee.

    Overall, he finished the season second on the team with 41 receptions for 475 yards and tied for third with two receiving touchdowns.

    Odell started 12 of 13 games for the Tigers in his sophomore year. He had his first college career punt return touchdown in a game against the North Texas Mean Green and his first college career game with over 100 yards against the Towson Tigers.

    He finished first on the team in receiving yards with 713, second in receptions with 43 and third with two receiving touchdowns.

    Beckham returned a failed field goal attempt for a record-long 109 yards and a touchdown in a match against UAB during his junior year.

    On October 26, he scored a college career-high 204 yards and two touchdowns against Furman Paladins.

    He was named the Paul Hornung Award winner for his exceptional performance as a wide receiver and return specialist, an award presented yearly to the most versatile player in major college football.

    ODB finished the season with 57 receptions, 1,117 yards, and eight touchdowns.

    He decided not to return to college for his senior year and entered the 2014 NFL Draft.

    Professional Career

    New York Giants

    Odell was selected with the 12th overall pick by the New York Giants in the first round of the 2014 NFL Draft. He signed a four-year rookie contract on May 19, 2014.

    He made his professional debut on October 5, 2014, against the Atlanta Falcons in Week 5 after missing most of the training camp, preseason, and first four games because of a hamstring injury.

    Odell recorded a one-handed touchdown reception, hailed as the “catch of the year,” on November 23, 2014, in a 31-28 loss against the Dallas Cowboys.

    The Pro Football Hall of Fame put the jersey he wore during his one-handed catch on display on December 8, 2014. 

    Since Odell scored at least 90 yards receiving in all five games in November, he set an NFL rookie record for a month and broke the rookie record.

    He became the first NFL rookie with at least 12 catches, 140 yards, and three scores in a game with 143 receiving yards, 12 catches, and three touchdowns on December 14, 2014.

    He tied for the most receptions in a game by a Giants rookie. Odell also eventually set an NFL rookie record and Giants record for recording over 90 receiving yards and most ever receptions in eight straight games.

    In a game against Philadelphia Eagles, Odell recorded a career-high 185 receiving yards, the most by a rookie in franchise history.

    He finished his rookie campaign with 91 receptions, 1,305 yards, and 12 touchdowns in 12 games.

    Fines During His Time With The Giants

    In a game against the Carolina Panthers during Week 15, Odell was involved in multiple confrontations with cornerback Josh Norman and was flagged for four penalties.

    He was suspended for one game without pay by the NFL Disciplinary Committee on December 21, 2015, for multiple violations of safety-related playing rules.

    The wide receiver finished the season with career-high of 1450 receiving yards and 13 touchdowns.

    During Week 1 on September 17, Odell was fined $12,154 for a dance celebration. During Week 2 on September 25, he was fined $36,000 for a blindside hit.

    He became the fastest player to record 200 receptions in only 30 games during Week 3. He started all 16 games and finished the season with 101 receptions, 1,367 receiving yards, and 10 touchdowns.

    Odell suffered an ankle sprain during the preseason and missed the season opener. He also suffered a fractured left ankle during Week 5 against the Los Angeles Chargers and was placed on an injured reserve.

    He finished the season with 25 receptions for 302 yards and three touchdowns.

    Giants Contract Extension

    Odell signed a five-year/$90 million contract with the Giants on August 27, 2018, including $41 million guaranteed.

    In Week 3, he became the player with the most receptions through his first 50 career games with 337 catches.

    Besides, he became the fourth player to record a passing touchdown of at least 50 yards while having at least 100 receiving yards in the same game.

    He became the first non-quarterback with multiple passing touchdowns in a season since Antwaan Randle El in 2010.

    He finished the season with 77 receptions, 1,052 yards, six touchdowns, and threw two passes for touchdowns.

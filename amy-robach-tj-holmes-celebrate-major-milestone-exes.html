    AMY Robach and TJ Holmes have shared a massive milestone for their new career venture on social media.

    This week, the news broke that the former GMA3 star's ex-spouses, Andrew Shue and Marilee Fiebig are dating.

    Now Amy, 50, and TJ, 46, have dropped major news of their own - surrounding their brand new self-named podcast.

    The former CNN reporter took to his Instagram Stories on Tuesday to share that the Amy and TJ Podcast had reached the number one spot on Apple.

    The TV personality showed his shock at the success of the program with a stunned-looking bitmoji.

    In a second post, TJ relaxed in a white bathrobe in bed with Amy for a sultry selfie.

    The couple gave the camera soft smiles as they cuddled together at home.

    The former ABC star wrote: "Thank you all," with a red heart emoji as he showed appreciation for fans listening to the podcast - resulting in the number one spot.

    SHOCKING TWIST

    TJ and Amy's success with their new career venture came just days after the news that the couple's exes had begun their own relationship.

    Marilee Fiebig and Andrew Shue, are now dating, Page Six first shared on Tuesday.

    The ABC alums were legally married to other people when their relationship was revealed - Amy to Melrose Place actor Andrew Shue, 56, and TJ to Marilee Fiebig, 46, with whom he shares 10-year-old daughter Sabine.

    The site reports their exes have now been dating for about six months after uniting over their shared divorces.

    "It turned into something else, and they’re connected over their values. It’s bigger than the affair now," one source said.

    And the two are reportedly already looking to the future.

    "They’re not heartbroken and sad,” said another source, adding: "Everyone has moved on."

    COMING CLEAN

    The new romance comes as the former Good Morning America 3 co-hosts just released the first installment of their new podcast, Amy & TJ, and already shared some shocking revelations about their romance.

    Amy and TJ made headlines last year for allegedly having an affair while co-hosting the daytime TV show.

    News broke of their secret relationship in November 2022, and they got the boot from ABC executives soon after.

    At one point in the new podcast episode, TJ noted that those close to the couple knew of their tight bond and often referred to them as "brother and sister."

    "A lot of people know. They used to call us brother and sister almost. Peas in a pod and all this stuff, to the point where when people would...make any kind of joke about what's going on with you two, we would almost go, 'Ew, that's like my sister, ugh gross!'" TJ recalled.

    Amy agreed that they didn't take well initially to comments about the two of them dating.

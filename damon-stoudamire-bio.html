    Damon Stoudamire | Professional Career

    Damon’s personal journey started when the Toronto Raptors selected him as the 7th overall pick in the 1995 NBA Draft.

    Subsequently, he went on to play three seasons for the team averaging 19.6 points, 8.8 assists, 4.2 rebounds, and 1.5 steals per game.

    To say the least, Stoudamire had a dream start to his career. In fact, he even won the 1996 NBA Rookie of the Year. Besides, he also earned a nomination in the NBA-All Rookie First Team.

    After that, the Raptors traded Damon to the Portland Trail Balzers in multiple-player trade.

    Expectations were high for the 5 feet 10 point guard to replicate his form and help the Trail Blazers win a Championship. But, on the contrary, Stoudamire never really managed to live up to the expectations.

    Thus, after seven years in Portland, Damon played for the Memphis Grizzlies and San Antonio Spurs before retiring in 2008.

    Coaching Career

    Damon started his coaching role as soon as he quit playing one. To explain, Stoudamire took up a coaching position as director of player development for Rice University.

    After that, he served as an assistant at Memphis Grizzlies, the University of Memphis, and the University of Arizona before settling down as the Pacific Tigers men’s basketball team’s head coach.

    BRUTAL Four In A Bed trolls have rejoiced after a 'petty and awful' contestant's hotel has closed down.

    The hit Channel 4 programme returned to screens on Monday with a brand new series.

    It sees four B&B owners throw open their doors to each other as they take turns spending a night at each business.

    Viewers watch as they mark their overnight stays based on hospitality and cleanliness.

    And during the first episode Maxine Marshall showed off her Greenhills Hotel in Wales.

    The businesswoman revealed it's a family affair with the majority of her clan working at the hotel.

    more on four in a bed

    However, the business now appears to have closed down, with an active proposal to strike it off the Companies House registrar.

    Potential guests are not able to book rooms at the Tenby location.

    Maxine ruffled feathers not just with her fellow contestants but with fans at home.

    She even stormed out off breakfast for being offered food with fat in it.

    Viewers took to social media in their droves to hit out at Maxine for demands and criticism.

    One said: "My heads starting to hurt with Maxine and her pettiness. They will not last till payment day i see them quitting before payment day."

    A second wrote: "Maxine started to cry and storm off because she got offered breakfast?"

    "Maxine is the peak of pettiness we expect from #fourinabed hope she carries on all week... absolutely cracks me up when people are so ridiculous," said a third.

    A fourth commented: "Sure any business would be glad to hear Maxine would not come back."

    While a fifth said: "Maxines place Greenhills is now permanently closed..she had a cheek calling it in Tenby."

    It remains to be seen whether Maxine will come out on top and win the Channel 4 series.

    Four in a Bed airs weekdays at 6pm on Channel 4.

    FLORIDA-based rapper Forgiato Blow became widely known because of his support of former President Donald Trump.

    Here's everything fans need to know about the controversial star.

    Who is rapper Forgiato Blow?

    Kurt Jantz is a popular rapper who goes by the stage name Forgiato Blow.

    He first rose to fame in the early 2020s because of his work in the Trumpist hip-hop subgenre, MAGA rap, also known as patriot rap and MAGA music, according to Vice.

    “I look at Trump like a rapper. A hustler. The man. Ballplayer. You know what I’m saying?” Jantz said during an interview with Vice. “He’s got everything he wants.”

    Since stepping into the music scene the avid Donald Trump supporter has released several songs, including Let's Go Brandon, Back the Blue, Boycott Target, Ride the Horse, and 4 More Years.

    Most of his songs focus on controversial topics facing society today, including calls to defund the police and Target's decision to remove some of its Pride merchandise.

    “I’ve always thought I was the Donald Trump of rap,” Jantz continued.

    “In the music industry, everyone loved me, but nobody wanted to support me on a big record label. Or everybody wants to support me and be my friend when they need something from me.

    "I felt like that was like Trump—before he was president, everybody loved him… Said he’s going to be the president and they said, ‘No way.’ And then what happened? He became the president.

    "That’s like me and music. I said I was going to make it through, you know, being a white rapper," Jantz added.

    When the self-proclaimed "Mayor Of Magaville" is not in the studio, he can often be found posting about his life on Twitter to over 133k followers.

    Who was Forgiato Blow's grandfather?

    While Jantz might be newer to the spotlight, it's nothing he's unfamiliar with.

    He grew up in a wealthy family thanks to the success of his grandfather, Stuart Arnold.

    Arnold was a highly successful businessman in the Tampa Bay area known for founding the magazine Auto-Trader.

    Along with founding the magazine, he also had several properties in the Tampa Bay and Clearwater area, including one that was once priced at more than $10million, according to the Tampa Bay Times.

    While Jantz's family might've had money, he insists that his career is entirely self-made.

    “People always say, ‘Oh, you had it easy, you were some spoiled, rich white kid who got homemade lollipops every day," he explained during his interview with Vice.

    While Jantz might insist that he's self-made, the exact details of his financial status are unclear at this time, but Vice reports that he drives two Rolls-Royces and often wears flashy clothes, including a pair of Louis Vuitton sneakers that retail for over $1,000.

    While Jantz might have a high-profile career, he often keeps details of his personal life out of the spotlight.

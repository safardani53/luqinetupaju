    PEAKY Blinders is set to return on Sunday night with plenty of fresh new drama.

    And in the first look at the new series, Tommy Shelby looks up to no good as he rides in on his horse.

    The clip shows the gangster- played by Cillian Murphy- riding across the fields dressed in his flat cap and usual smart attire as the faint sound of a ringing phone can be heard in the background.

    But as Tommy gets off the horse and walks towards the striking red abandoned phone box, he says: "Arthur?"

    As keen fans of the crime based period drama will know, Arthur is the name of Tommy's older brother- who made a shocking return during the series four finale to kill Luca Changretta.

    The show is set to make its' highly anticipated return after a two year wait.

    And series five looks set to be intense, with a trailer that was released last month showing an exploding house and even teasing a major character's death.

    Tommy speaks over the top of gripping scenes during the one-clip, saying: "There is God and there is the Peaky Blinders.

    "We own the ropes. Who's going to hang us now, eh?"

    In the most fiery scenes of the series' history, Tommy is seen holding a gun at someone hung up on a crucifix before a massive explosion takes them both out.

    His sister Ada is seen in floods of tears before telling him: "No one's going to hang you Tommy, you're going to hang yourself."

    At the end of series four, Tommy was elected to represent South Birmingham as a Member of Parliament.

    He looks to have soon made enemies with Sam Claflin's Oswald Mosley, leader of the British Union of Fascists who says: "Mr Shelby, you've come to my attention. England lives tonight and marches on."

    Meanwhile, Aunt Polly is seen holding up a handgun and her son Michael Gray's got a new woman.

    The explosive trailer ends with the haunting line: "Sometimes death is a kindness."

    According to Cillian Murphy fans of the hit BBC drama can expect plenty more action - and a lot more violence - from the Shelby clan.

    Series five will see plenty of twists and turns, with Tommy set to lose a lot of money in the Wall Street Crash.

    "It's 1929, the time of the Wall Street Crash, and things go badly wrong for the Shelbys," Cillian told Heat magazine.

    "They lose a lot of money. So, that's where we find him. He's an MP and it's two years after we last met him."

    He also revealed that Arthur (Paul Anderson) will be back to his violent best.

    "He's still the same. His relationship with Linda is on a knife edge, and there's a couple of things that happen that force the animal within - as he he calls it in the show - to emerge quite forcefully," he said.

    And speaking of the famous family's plans for the new series, Cillian revealed: "The family is as complicated as always, and success and wealth brings its own baggage.

    "And I think with Tommy trying to balance being an MP and a legitimate businessman, alongside being the leader of a criminal operation, makes for a brilliant drama."

    Peaky Blinders returns to BBC One at 9PM on Sunday

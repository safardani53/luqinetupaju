    Daniel Joe Peters is Mudhoney's drummer. He joined the Bundle of Hiss at the age of fifteen. He also played the drums for Nirvana, appearing on a single, "Sliver". Peters' only live appearance with Nirvana was on September 22, 1990 in Seattle, Washington, at the Motor Sports International Garage. Peters went on to play drums for Mudhoney and was replaced at Nirvana by Dave Grohl. Peters expressed his only regret that he missed a chance to play the drums on Nevermind. He returned to Mudhoney to record Every Good Boy Deserves Fudge. He's been with Mudhoney ever since. Mudhoney's latest product, Vanishing Point, was released on April 2, 2013. Peters also played drums for Ellensburg, Washington's Screaming Trees band from 1990 to 1991. He also appeared in Black Sheep alongside David Spade and Chris Farley. He is married to a prominent Seattle-based Emerald Law Group attorney. Previously, she was an associate at the respectable law firm of Scott, Kinney & Mack pllc.

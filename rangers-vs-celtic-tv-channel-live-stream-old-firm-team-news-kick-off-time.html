    RANGERS and Celtic go head-to-head in the first Old Firm clash of the season with the Gers already five points adrift of their big rivals.

    Brendan Rodgers hasn't suffered a Scottish Premiership defeat since taking over at Celtic and the Bhoys are unbeaten in this famous and fierce derby since 2012.


    They crushed Dundee 4-0 in the Scottish League Cup mid-week and have only lost twice in their last 15 games - to Astana and PSG in the Champions League.

    Rangers have started the season well, tasting defeat only once to Hibs, but slipped up last time out in the league, drawing 2-2 with ten-man Partick.

    What time is kick-off?

    THE game takes place on Saturday 23 September at Ibrox.

    Things get underway at 12pm.

    Which TV channel can I watch it on?

    YOU can watch the game on Sky Sports Main Event, with coverage starting at 11.30am.

    Or you can stay with us here on SunSport and follow the action with our LIVE blog.

    What is the team news?

    RANGERS have a number of injury worries ahead of the big game on Saturday lunchtime.

    Lee Wallace definitely misses the game, whilst there are doubts over Bruno Alves and Declan John after they limped out of the midweek League Cup win.

    But Rodgers has his own problems, with Jozo Simunovic still nursing a knock and Erik Sviatchenko a long-term absentee.

    Young midfielder Kouassi Eboue could feature but will be a game-time decision.

    WALMART has brought back the 90s cult classic Office Space in a bid to shake-up Black Friday and bring customers more bargains.

    The retailer is reviving the 1999 comedy for a new set of ads - hoping to help Americans who may have a case of "the Mondays".

    The move will see bargains pushed at the start of each week and will help draw out Black Friday for the whole of the holiday season.

    Casey Schlaybaugh, Walmart’s vice president of brand marketing and strategy, said: “We know customers want to shop for deals earlier, and they want to shop for deals often."

    She said the “Office Space” ads will surface each Monday in November, appearing on TV and ads in movie theaters.

    And many of the sales and bargains will show up online — at the start of each week.

    The Monday blues theme featured heavily in the Office Space movie, which sees character Peter Gibbons navigate the rules and regulations of life at company Initech. 

    Walmart's campaign will feature actors Gary Cole, who played the infamous corporate manager Bill Lumbergh and Ajay Naidu, who played the rebellious tech worker Samir Nagheenanajar.

    Shoppers may also set get a surprise from the movie's character Michael Bolton, played by actor David Herman.

    It comes as the retialed halves its Walmart+ membership fee for those who sign up in the next two days.

    Manchester Airport terror alert sparked by hoax caller reporting 'suspicious package' on Thomas Cook

    BOMB cops stormed a plane after receiving an anonymous call about a suspicious package on board which turned out to be a hoax. Panicked passengers were stranded for over four hours as security teams scoured the Thomas Cook flight at Manchester Airport on Thursday.

    23 Aug 2024, 00:00

    BOMB cops stormed a plane after receiving an anonymous call about a suspicious package on board – which turned out to be a hoax.

    Panicked passengers were stranded for over four hours as security teams scoured the Thomas Cook flight at Manchester Airport on Thursday.

    Authorities were reacting to a phone call, which claimed Flight MT578 to Hurghada, Egypt, was under threat.

    However officers eventually ruled there was no foul play and allowed travellers were given the green light to get back on board.

    This evening Pat Barry, from Leigh, Lancashire, told The Sun: "I'm still sitting in Manchester Airport with absolutely no update from Thomas Cook, no idea when I'll get on that flight.

    "I've been here since 10 o'clock this morning, we got on the flight, then we were told to get off the flight.

    The 52-year-old said: "There were no updates whatsoever from Thomas Cook, it's been an absolute shambles."

    A Greater Manchester Police spokesman said: “At around 2:40pm police received a report that a suspicious package was on board a plane at Manchester Airport.

    “As a precaution passengers were taken off the flight whilst enquiries were carried out.

    “These enquiries are coming to a conclusion and nothing suspicious has been found.”

    The flight was due to take off at 2.20pm, while thousands of other passengers were hit by delays as Storm Doris caused chaos at the airport.

    Gary Gray, of South Shields, Tyne and Wear, was caught up in the drama while preparing to jet off on a ten-day jolly with his wife.

    He told The Sun: “We were all on board when the message came on the intercom that everyone had to get off.

    “We were told very little but when I asked a flight attendant she said there’s been a major security breach.

    “When I asked what she meant by that she said she couldn’t say. But everyone was very worried.

    “There’s been a lot of delays at the airport due to the bad weather, but we could tell this was something a lot more serious.

    “My wife’s a nervous flier and this has made things a lot worse. Thankfully nothing bad was found.

    “We’ve spent £1600 on this trip – luckily it’s all inclusive so I’ll be needing a good strong drink when I arrive after this.”

    A Thomas Cook spokesman added: “After being contacted by police, all customers and baggage were rescreened and the aircraft was given an additional security search.

    “Safety is always our first priority. We’re sorry for the inconvenience this has caused.”

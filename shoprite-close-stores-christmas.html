    DON'T be caught in the wrong by knocking on ShopRite's doors next Monday.

    You won't have much luck, as ShopRite stores will bolt doors on December 25 for Christmas.

    RITE TO CLOSE

    This will free up time for employees to join in on holiday festivities.

    They're still expected to work on Christmas Eve though, but with reduced hours until 7 pm.

    For the day after Christmas, it's back to usual business.

    The store will also retain its regular hours on New Year's Eve.

    So you can stop there for last-minute party grabs before the Times Square ball drops.

    Most stores stay open until 10 pm or 11 pm on business days and weekends. Some are even open till midnight.

    But be sure to check ShopRite's store locator to confirm your shop's specific hours, as hours will vary by location.

    Make sure you don't miss out on all their deals and coupons.

    From sugar cookie truffles to gingerbread lattes, peppermint bark, and banana pudding, ShopRite offers a wide array of festive recipes at affordable prices.

    The banana pudding fits into a $6 budget, while the sugar cookie truffles cost only $4.

    There's also a holiday ham you can snag for $11 that feeds 8 people.

    You can view the full selection of recipes on ShopRite's website.

    MERRY MADNESS

    ShopRite joins supermarkets and retail chains across the country who are closing shop to celebrate Kris Kringle and give workers a day off.

    Walmart, Costco, Target, Trader Joe's, and Sam's Club will be closed this year on December 25 as they usually are.

    If you're a book worm, make sure you grab your latest read on the day before or after Christmas from Barnes and Noble.

    While Dollar Tree is closed for Christmas, you'll be able to stop in the night before.

    If you need to make any last-minute food runs, you can count on most 7-Elevens or Albertsons, but make sure you call beforehand.

    Check if your local ShopRite has been shut down for good.

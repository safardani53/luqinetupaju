    Barry Sanders: College Career 

    Sanders’ college career started with his enrollment to play football at Oklahoma State University. He wore the jersey No. 21 for the Oklahoma State Cowboys from 1986 to 1988.

    During Barry’s first two years, he backed up All-American Thurman Thomas. Likewise, in 1987, Barry led the nation in yards per kickoff return (31.6).

    At the same time, he also rushed for 600 yards and scored eight touchdowns, including four on special teams returns.

    Later, Thurman moved on to the NFL, and Barry became the starter for his junior year.

    The American football player led the country in 1988 in what is regarded as one of the exemplary individual seasons in college football history.

    He averaged 7.6 yards per run and over 200 yards per game, including rushing for over 300 yards in four games.

    Further, Barry also set collegiate football season records with 2,628 yards running, 3,248 total yards, and, 234 points. He also had 39 touchdowns and 37 rushing touchdowns.

    Further, he played five consecutive 200-yard games, at least two touchdowns in 11 straight games, and at least three touchdowns nine times.

    Likewise, in the 1988 Holiday Bowl, Barry also ran for 222 yards and scored five touchdowns in his three-quarters of action. Holiday Bowl is a game that is not included in the official NCAA season statistics.

    Because of his notable achievements, Barry was recognized as the most outstanding college player in the nation. As a result, Sanders was awarded the Heisman Trophy in 1998. 

    Barry was in Tokyo, Japan, with his team during the Heisman Trophy win. His team was preparing to face Texas Tech in the Coca-Cola Classic.

    Barry Sanders left Oklahoma State before his senior season to start his NFL career. 

    Barry Sanders: Professional Career 

    In the 1989 NFL Draft, The Detroit Lions selected the Heisman Trophy winner as their 3rd overall pick. Barry owes his selection to the then-coach of the Lions, Wayne Fontes. 

    The management team of the Detroit Lions had considered drafting Deion Sanders instead of Barry Sanders. However, Wayne realized Barry’s potential and convinced the team to draft Barry Sanders.

    Barry Sanders was given Jersey No. 20. Billy Sims and Lem Barney had previously worn the number. They were among the greatest players that the Lions ever had. 

    Further, in the early 1980s, Billy Sims was one of the league’s best running backs. So, Wayne Fontes requested Barry to wear the number in order to pay tribute to Billy Sims. 

    1997 was Barry Sanders’ greatest season when he became a 2,000 running yards club member.

    The American player rushed for 14 straight 100-yard games for an NFL record, including two 200-yard efforts.

    He became only the third player in NFL history to run for 2,000 yards in a single season. Likewise, he was the first player since O. J. Simpson ran for 2000 yards in 14 back-to-back games.

    1998 was Barry’s last season in NFL. In his last season in the NFL, Sanders ran for 1,491 yards and, unfortunately, ended his four-year streak of running for over 1,500 yards in a season.

    10 years of NFL career was rewarding to Barry as he achieved the greatest heights of success. In spite of his individual success, he could not lead the Detroit Lions to the Super Bowl during his stay at the Lions.

    Retirement

    On July 27, 1999, Barry Sanders announced that he was retiring from his career as a professional football player.

    Barry’s retirement was made public by faxing a letter to his hometown newspaper, the Wichita Eagle.

    In the letter, the American player wrote:

    “Shortly after the end of last season, I felt that I probably would not return for the 1999–2000 season. I also felt that I should take as much time as possible to sort through my feelings and make sure that my feelings were back with conviction. Today, I officially declare my departure from the NFL.”

    He left NFL with tons of achievements. He had run 15 269 yards in his 10-year career. It is the highest total rushing yards ever by any NFL player in a 10-year time. 

    Barry Sander’s retirement was very unexpected and controversial. There were many theories on why the player decided to leave such a fulfilling career. 

    However, later it became known that he willingly retired and was not forced by anyone to do so. His issues with the management team and direction of the Detroit Lions and the resulting lack of success also contributed to his retirement. 

    Barry Sanders has always appeared in various Lions’ events despite Barry’s disappointment with the Lions.

    Further, Barry also rejoined the Lions in a paid role as the team’s ambassador in 2017. Therefore, we can say that things have now patched up between Barry and the Lions.

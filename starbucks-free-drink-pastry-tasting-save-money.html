    STARBUCKS serves nearly 40million customers in the US every month - and you could be one of them who gets a free drink.

    While most of us are familiar with Starbucks' vast coffee selection and somewhat confusing sizes, there are aspects of the menu that employees know better than shoppers.

    With more than 15,000 locations nationwide, Starbucks is a well-known staple no matter where you live.

    One former Starbucks barista shared a little-known trick that can get customers a free drink.

    Cody Hodsgon, who goes by cody_hodgson on TikTok, shared a video on his page in late 2020 advising consumers to ask their barista for samples.

    You need to be in a Starbucks location to try a tasting, so mobile or drive-through orders won't work.

    READ MORE ON STARBUCKS

    Coffee samples

    According to Cody, all you need to do is ask the barista to do a coffee tasting.

    Availability may change based on how crowded your Starbucks is, however.

    Other baristas said in the comments of Cody's video that they love the practice, but have to turn down customers if the store is packed.

    Most read in Money

    A Starbucks spokesperson told The Sun: "We encourage customers to ask their barista to help them find the right food and beverage items for their taste preference.

    "Our barista’s expertise in helping customers find the right menu item has and always will be at the heart of the Starbucks experience."

    However, the spokesperson denied that Starbucks offers food and beverage tastings to customers upon request.

    The Starbucks spokesperson explained that "Starbucks stores may offer customers samples in a tasting cup; however, sample offerings will vary by location, season, and a store’s ability to accommodate based on their operations."

    Cody shared a second tip in his video, as well - less about saving money, and more about ensuring your trip to Starbucks goes smoothly.

    He simply advises customers to not be rude to their baristas to ensure their drink comes out properly and as ordered.

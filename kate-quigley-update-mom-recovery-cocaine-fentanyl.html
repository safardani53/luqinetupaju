    COMEDIAN Kate Quigley's family have given an update on her health after she reportedly took fentanyl-laced cocaine at a party in LA, saying she is &quot;stable&quot; but isn't lucid &quot;for more than a moment at a time.&quot;

    12 Jul 2024, 00:00

    COMEDIAN Kate Quigley's family have given an update on her health after she reportedly took fentanyl-laced cocaine at a party in LA, saying she is "stable" but isn't lucid "for more than a moment at a time."

    The 39-year-old stage star was found unresponsive by police inside the plush $1.8 million apartment she rents on the Venice Canals early Saturday morning.

    Fellow comic Fuquan Johnson, 42, was pronounced dead at the scene alongside stand-up Enrico Colangeli, 48, and Quigley's 33-year-old friend Natalie Williamson.

    Investigators believe the four friends all consumed a batch of cocaine that had been laced with fentanyl, a powerful synthetic opioid that is 50 times more potent than heroin.

    Quigley's mom, Fran Wyles, who lives in Ohio, gave family and friends an update on her condition, writing on Facebook: "Thank you everyone for your prayers and positivity. Kate is stable.

    "She still isn’t lucid for more than a moment at a time. We are optimistic that she will recover (tho it won't be quick).

    "She is unable to post (contrary to reports) but we are hoping she might be able to tomorrow. Again, thank you."

    A second relative, who wishes not to be named, told The Sun: "We thank you and everyone for their thoughts and prayers.

    "Kate is recovering and our focus is on getting her healthy again.

    "We respect Kate's privacy and therefore won't go into details. She is lucky to be alive, and she has a tough road ahead."

    Quigley has still not posted anything publicly on social media regarding her recovery, but allegedly texted a friend hours after being admitted to hospital.

    “I'm alive. I'm not great. But I'm ok," she is said to have told comedian pal, Brian Redban, after he asked how she was following the tragedy.

    Redban shared a screenshot of the Saturday night text exchange on Instagram, captioning the story: "I have had a bad month.. And this just ruined me.. Hug everyone you love."

    Police responded to a 911 call at her plush rented apartment in Venice Canals, with photographs obtained by The Sun showing her luxury property following the deadly party over the weekend.

    Autopsies will be carried out on Johnson, Colangeli, and Williamson this week to confirm their official causes of death, while the Los Angeles stand-up community has been in mourning.

    Dealers have reportedly turned to fentanyl to keep their supply of drugs flowing throughout the pandemic.

    And in a haunting tweet on the Thursday before the apparent overdose, Johnson said: "I'm so HIGH I just TRIED to SHAMPOO my body."

    Police were directed to a home in the 200 block of Carroll Canal where a number of people are listed, although neighbors say the deadly party went down next door, where Quigley has lived for around a year.

    One told The Sun: "I saw some people arrive in the afternoon but all the doors were closed and it wasn't noisy, I found out when I woke up what had happened.

    "I didn't know the people involved but have seen Kate around for the past year, it's such a sad, sad situation.

    "I've heard since being taken to hospital she is doing well and will make a full recovery. I hope so."

    Fellow comedian Jack Assadourian Jr. told The Sun that he will be hosting a memorial for Johnson and Colangeli in LA on Thursday to commemorate the two men, both of whom "had a heart of gold", he said.

    The service will be held at the HaHa Comedy Club in North Hollywood, which is owned by Assadourian's parents and severed as the backdrop for countless of the two funnymen's shows.

    Calling Johnson and Colangeli "two of the most important people" in his life, Assadourian, 36, said he had known the pair for more than a decade and at one time lived with them both.

    "We did shows together, toured together, spent holidays together, birthdays and major events - or even it was just on Monday," Assadourian added. "We were a family."

    26 May 2024, 00:00

    JENNIFER Lopez and fiancé Alex Rodriguez joined thousands of people in Hollywood yesterday for the Black Lives Matter protest.

    The 50-year-old was still recognizable despite being clad in an oversized black hoodie and sporting a black-sequin face mask due to the coronavirus.

    The Let's Get Loud songstress teamed her oversized sweater with a pair of black joggers and white trainers as she carried a sign with blue disposable gloves that read "Black Live Matter."

    New York native Jennifer wore her long in a high ponytail as she sported a pair of oversized shades.

    Complementing the singer, former Yankees star Alex, 44, also donned a black face mask and hoodie for the march.

    The sports star sported a pair of aviator-style shades while he carried a homemade sign with white disposable gloves that read "Let's get loud for Black Lives Matter."

    Jennifer and Alex also took to Instagram to share several shots from the protest; including a number of them holding their signs.

    The A-list couple weren't the only famous faces to join the march, which was reported to be the largest protest yet in Southern Californian with an estimated 50,000 taking to the streets, according to LAPD's Hollywood Division.

    Oscar-winner Jamie Foxx walked in the protest, as well as actress Vanessa Hudgens, model Kaia Gerber and Riverdale star Cole Sprouse.

    Rapper YG, model Stella Maxwell and Jeremy Meeks were seen marching while Machine Gun Kelly was spotted handing out free snacks and water to protesters.

    The march passed off peacefully as the marchers made their way past Hollywood landmarks including the Capitol Records building, the Kodak Theatre and along the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

    MOST READ IN ENTERTAINMENT

    The Hollywood march came as peaceful demonstrations continued across America two weeks in response to the death of George Floyd in Minneapolis.

    George died after former Minneapolis Police Officer Derek Chauvin, 44, knelt on the man’s neck for nearly nine minutes while George was face down on the ground with his hands cuffed behind his back and pleaded that he couldn't breathe.

    Chauvin has been charged with second-degree murder while the other three arresting officers; J.Alexander Keung, Thomas Lane, and Tou Thao, have been charged with aiding and abetting second-degree murder.

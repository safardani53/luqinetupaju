    Who is Amanda Plummer?

    Amanda Michael Plummer is a stage and film actress who was born on March 23, 1957 in New York City.

    She is the only daughter of Hollywood icons Christopher Plummer and Tammy Grimes.

    The 63-year-old actress first gained recognition for her breakthrough role in The Fisher King, which she starred in opposite the late Robin Williams in 1991.

    Plummer is best known for her work in Quentin Tarantino's 1994 classic Pulp Fiction, where she stars as one of two robbers who hold up a restaurant.

    Tarantino has said that he wrote the part specifically for Plummer and her on-screen partner-in-crime Tim Roth.

    She's since appeared in a variety of stage plays and films, including The Prophecy (1995), Freeway (1996), and My Life Without Me (2003).

    Amanda Plummer has reportedly only been in a handful of relationships and has never married.

    It is also understood she was in a relationship with Lawrence of Arabia actor Peter O'Toole between 1979 and 1982.

    She does not have any children.

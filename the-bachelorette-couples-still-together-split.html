    SINCE premiering in 2003, The Bachelorette has seen a slew of contestants try and find their prince charming.

    However, not all of the winners have stayed together, causing fans to wonder who from the Bachelorette franchise is still coupled up.

    Which couples from The Bachelorette are still together?

    The Bachelorette couples that are still together include:

    Trista Rehn and Ryan Sutter, Season 1

    Trista Rehn made history in 2003 after becoming the first Bachelorette in history.

    After finishing as the runner-up on season one of The Bachelor, she found her match on The Bachelorette.

    During the first season, she met Ryan Sutter, and the two have been together ever since.

    Now, the couple lives in Colorado with their two kids, Maxwell and Blakesley.

    Trista also appeared on Dancing with the Stars and Fear Factor after her iconic run in the Bachelor franchise.

    Desiree Hartsock and Chris Siegfried, Season 9

    After getting eliminated in week eight of The Bachelor, Desiree Hartsock went on to star in her own season of The Bachelorette.

    At first, many didn't think the two would make it, but they have ultimately proved the haters wrong.

    Most read in Entertainment

    After getting engaged, the two tied the knot and have since welcomed two sons, Asher and Zander.

    JoJo Fletcher and Jordan Rodgers, Season 12

    JoJo Fletcher quickly found love following her run on The Bachelor.

    During season 12, she met former pro quarterback Jordan Rodgers and then got engaged in 2016.

    Because of the Covid-19 pandemic, the two had to postpone their wedding but were able to finally tie the knot in 2022.

    JoJo and Jordan got married on May 14, 2022, six years after getting engaged on the show.

    Charity Lawson and Dotun Olubeko, Season 20

    Fans saw Charity Lawson fall in love with Dotun Olubeko on the show's 20th season.

    Since popping the question, Olubeko and Lawson have been living together in Los Angeles.

    Lawson recently competed on Dancing With The Stars, finishing in fourth place.

    In a recent Instagram story, Lawson also revealed that the two's engagement will be a long one, saying she expects their wedding to happen sometime in 2025.

    Lawson, a child and family therapist, and Olubeko, an integrative medicine specialist, are taking their relationship – and upcoming marriage – as seriously as they can.

    In November 2015, Lawson shared that the duo are in couple's therapy, telling Life and Style: "Our therapist is personally, like, really proud of us because we don’t really have, like, problems."

    “It’s just, like, we’re doing things to be proactive, to, like, stay ahead of, like, I guess something going wrong in the future," she said.

    Recent Split: Rachel Lindsay and Bryan Abasolo, Season 13

    During season 13, Rachel Lindsay made history as the show's first Black Bachelorette.

    While making history, she also found herself true love after falling for contestant Bryan Abasolo.

    Following their on-air engagement in 2017, the two tied the knot at a destination wedding in Cancún in August 2019.

    Unfortunately, after four years of marriage, Abasolo filed for divorce.

    He announced the news on his Instagram on January 2, 2024, writing: "After more than 4 years of marriage, Rachel and I have made the difficult decision to part ways and start anew."

    "My parents have been married forever and I'm a family man, but sometimes loving yourself and your partner means you must let go."

    "I wanted you to hear it from the source before the blogs start making up their own reality."

    "Please respect the spaces of our family and friends as we figure out our next steps," he added.

    How many seasons of The Bachelorette are there?

    The Bachelorette is one of the most successful spin-offs of The Bachelor franchise.

    The dating game show premiered on January 8, 2003.

    Since then, the show has aired 224 episodes across 20 seasons.

    The Bachelorette aired annually in 2003, 2004, and 2005, before taking a brief hiatus.

    The show returned to ABC in the spring of 2008 and has aired at least once per year ever since.

    The Bachelor franchise has also led to several other spin-off series, including Bachelor Pad, Bachelor in Paradise, The Bachelor Winter Games, and The Golden Bachelor.

    MEET Colossus, the venom-ridden spider that could kill you in just 15 minutes.

    The giant funnel web has just been handed in to the Australian Reptile Park, the second biggest the park has ever seen.

    Aptly named, the eight-legged monster measures 7.8cm across and was found on the Central Coast in New South Wales.

    Staff at the park have found the creature's fangs are constantly dripping with venom when it rears its furry legs.

    But the venomous creature could actually save lives after it was donated to the wildlife sanctuary as part of their anti-venom programme.

    Staff collect samples of snake and spider venom to help develop life-saving funnel anti-venom, a treatment discovered in 1981.

    The programme involves specialist staff milking the spiders of their venom and sending it to Seqirus, a vaccine manufacturer in Melbourne.

    The average funnel web needs to be "milked" up to 70 times to produce a single dose.

    Australian funnel web spiders - the facts

    • They are one of the most dangerous groups of spiders in the world
    • The venomous beasts can grow up to 10cm in size
    • A singel funnel web is packed with so much venom it can kill a human in 15 minutes
    • They can survive immersion for several hours and bite when removed from the water
    • Symptoms of a giant funnel web bite include goose bumps, sweating, tingling around the mouth and increased heart rate and blood pressure
    • No fatalities have been recorded since 1981 though, when anti-venom was invented
    • Spiders like Colossus can be 'milked', producing a substance that can be developed into anti-venom
    • Bites from Sydney funnel web spiders have caused 13 deaths, seven in children

    The park's Head curator of the Australian Reptile Park, Liz Gabriel, warns that this time of year is peak season for funnel webs - particularly in such areas as the laundry, or anywhere that's humid and damp.

    "Weather we've seen over the last week is particularly attractive to funnel webs," she said.

    "They love damp environments so the rain will make them wander about more."

    Gabriel also offers some advice for anyone who is bitten by a funnel web.

    "Stay as calm as possible and apply the correct first aid, which is a pressure immobilisation bandage,' she said.

    "And get to hospital as fast as you possibly can."
